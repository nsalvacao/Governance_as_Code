## Product Goal / Outcome Statement — `{{PRODUCT_OR_INITIATIVE_NAME}}`

This document defines the long-term direction (Product Vision), the measurable intermediate state the team is targeting (Product Goal), and the Objectives and Key Results that translate the goal into verifiable outcomes. It tracks outcomes, not feature lists — a shipped feature is not an outcome until it produces a measurable change in user or business behaviour.

---

### Product Vision

> `{{PRODUCT_VISION}}`

One sentence describing the aspirational future state of the product. It should be stable over years. The vision is the north star against which all Product Goals are evaluated.

---

### Product Goal

> `{{PRODUCT_GOAL}}`

The Product Goal is the single measurable intermediate state the Scrum Team is pursuing. Per the Scrum Guide 2020: *"The Product Goal describes a future state of the product which can serve as a target for the Scrum Team to plan against."* There is only one Product Goal at a time. The current Product Backlog exists to achieve it.

---

### Objective

> `{{OBJECTIVE}}`

A qualitative statement of what the team wants to achieve in the current planning horizon. It should be inspiring, action-oriented, and time-bound. Objectives do not contain numbers — numbers live in Key Results.

---

### Key Results

Key Results measure progress toward the Objective. They must be specific, measurable, and time-bounded. Each KR should be an outcome indicator, not an output or activity.
