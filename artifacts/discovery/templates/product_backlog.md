## Product Backlog — `{{PRODUCT_OR_INITIATIVE_NAME}}`

### Product Goal

> `{{PRODUCT_GOAL}}`

The Product Goal is the single long-term objective for the Scrum Team. It describes a future state of the product that can serve as a target for planning. The Product Backlog exists to achieve the Product Goal. There is only one Product Goal at a time.

---

### Product Backlog Items

Each item must have enough detail to be pulled into a Sprint. Items near the top of the backlog must be refined to the point they meet the Definition of Ready (small enough, understood, estimated, testable). Items further down may remain coarse.

**Type values:** Story / Task / Spike / Bug

**Priority values (MoSCoW):** Must / Should / Could / Won't

**Status values:** Backlog / Refined / In Sprint / Done

> Each item must meet the Definition of Done before it can be considered complete. The Definition of Done is a shared standard applied to every Product Backlog item selected for a Sprint.

---

### Sprint Goal (Current Sprint)

> `{{SPRINT_GOAL}}`

The Sprint Goal is the single objective for the Sprint. It communicates why the Sprint is valuable. It is committed to by the entire Scrum Team during Sprint Planning. The Sprint Goal should provide focus and flexibility.

---

### Refinement Notes

`{{REFINEMENT_NOTES}}`

Backlog refinement is the ongoing act of adding detail, estimates, and order to Product Backlog items. Record key decisions, split actions, and clarifications reached during refinement sessions here. Refinement is not a formal Scrum event but must happen continuously so the backlog is Sprint-ready.

---

### Ordering Rules

- Keep the highest-value, highest-risk, or highest-uncertainty items near the top.
- Re-order when evidence changes, not only on a fixed cadence.
- Link each item to a discovery brief, experiment, or decision record.
- The Product Owner is solely accountable for the order of the Product Backlog.
