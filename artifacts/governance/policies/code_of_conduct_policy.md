## Standards

### Expected Behaviors

All community members are expected to:

- Demonstrate empathy and kindness toward other people.
- Be respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences.
- Give and gracefully accept constructive feedback.
- Accept responsibility and apologize to those affected by mistakes; learn from the experience.
- Focus on what is best for the overall community, not just individual benefit.

### Unacceptable Behaviors

The following behaviors are not tolerated in any community space:

- The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing others' private information (physical or email addresses) without explicit permission.
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

`{{ENFORCEMENT_TEAM}}` is responsible for clarifying and enforcing the standards of acceptable behavior. They will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they determine to be inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

`{{ENFORCEMENT_TEAM}}` has the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this policy, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Enforcement Guidelines — 4-Tier Ladder

Community leaders follow these guidelines when determining consequences for violations. Severity is assessed by `{{ENFORCEMENT_TEAM}}` based on the nature, frequency, and impact of the violation.

### Tier 1 — Correction

**Trigger**: A first-time, low-severity violation such as inappropriate language or behavior deemed unprofessional.

**Action**: A private, written warning from `{{ENFORCEMENT_TEAM}}` providing clarity around the nature of the violation and why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### Tier 2 — Warning

**Trigger**: A violation through a single incident or a series of actions after a Tier 1 correction.

**Action**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. A temporary interaction ban is imposed: no interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### Tier 3 — Temporary Ban

**Trigger**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Action**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for `{{TEMP_BAN_DURATION}}`. No public or private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with `{{ENFORCEMENT_TEAM}}`, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### Tier 4 — Permanent Ban

**Trigger**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment, or aggression toward any individual or group.

**Action**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.
